Twelve Pillars

By: Jim Rohn and Chris Widener


Summary of Chapter 4

Chapter four starts off with Charlie, an expert talking to Micheal about life. Charlie said the phrase: "Just remember that once a flower blooms, it still needs water and sun to keep it from wilting.” This resonated with my own personal life where I have to keep working on Myself and the progress i've made to ensure I don't fall backwards in life.


Charlie was educating Micheal about setting goals because Michael felt like he was not living up to his potential. Not having goals was the reason Michael felt this way and was achieving little. Charlie gives Michael advice to write his goals down to bring it into reality. The final outcome of your goals should be to make you a better person. There are long term and short term goals. Michael wishes he was where he wanted to be already which Charlie replied with: 'You cannot change your destination overnight, but you can change your direction overnight.' This resonated with me and showed me that the first step is to stop wishing and just start. changing my direction. Start with micro goals that leads to the final goal. This will help you get there a little at a time without feeling too overwhelmed. According to Charlie in the book, 'The most powerful lesson in life is to just decide what you want to do and get started.'


Summary of Chapter 5

Chapter 5 starts off with Michael making excuses about completing a goal in his daily life. Charlie informed him that he has to make a chouce between discipline or regret. Regret is way worse when you are in the last days of your life and can't do the things you wanted to while discipline is small pains you go through daily to achieve what you want in life and feel accomplished.


Charlie told Michael that he should spend his days wisely because days are expensive and as each day goes by you have one less day to spend. Charlie talks about not allowing the days to enslave us but to take charge and be the designer of our lives. This taught me to not just get by each day but to plan my day and organize my time to get things done and take every opportunity that comes my way that will help me achieve my overall goals. Either you control your time or your time controls you.


Summary of Chapter 6

Chapter 6 is about growth and acquaintances. This chapter reminds me of going to highschool and every devotion, my principal would say 'surround yourself with people and things that are good for you.' Charlie is telling Michael to surround himself with successful people and winners whho exhibit the values and skills hhe wants to acquire. Ask yourself questions about who you hang around and how they affect your way of thinnking and time management then ask, am I okay with that? If the effect your firends have on you are negative, then it is up to you to make the decision to protect yourselves from the wrong influences. Charlie gives excellent advice to categorize your relationships and ask yourself if each person is someone you should stop, limit or expand your associations with.


Think about the time you spend with each person and manage your time to see the amount of time you need with each person that will challenge you to become the kind of person you wish to be. Be who you want to attract. Charlie taught Michael that if he works on himself and become successful, then it will be easier to be in a relationship with successful people. Open yourslef up to new knowledge. Charlie stated that 99% of life is attitude which is greatly shaped by influences and association. If the people who you spend time with don’t have attitudes that are positive, they will rub off on you and keep you from becoming all that you can be. As Charlie said, 'When the student is ready, the teacher will appear.' I take this to mean, when we are open to changing our direction in life, the resources and people we need will be there for us.


Summary of Chapter 7

Accroding to Charlie, a successful person is a lifelong learner. Formal education will make you a living but Self Education will make you a fortune. It is important to understand This phrase to mean that going to school will only allow you to earn money to live life but your personal growth will remain stagnant. Self-Education is what you teach yourself after formal education and is what you learn along the way so that you are constantly improving and growing. if we are going down the wrong path, education is what we need to turn us around in the right direction. The first step is to read books then learn by observation from successful people. The third step is to reflect on your own experiences to see what went right vs what went wrong.


Books may be expensive but it is worth the cost with what we will learn from them. When we read books we open ourselves to new worlds to explore and understand. Reading books gives us access to successful people's mindsets that we can apply to our own lives to achieve our goals and be successful ourselves.



Conclusion

These few chapters teaches us to start, set goals, manage our time wisely, surround ourselves with positive successful people and to be a lifelong learner. I can apply all these to my personal growth and development so that I do not feel stuck in life.
